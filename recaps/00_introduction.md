# House of Leaves — Foreword & Introduction Recap

---

## Structure Overview

The book has **three voices layered on top of each other**:
1. **Zampanô** — blind, dead old man who wrote the original academic analysis of "The Navidson Record"
2. **Johnny Truant** — the young editor who found Zampanô's manuscript and is compiling/annotating it (footnotes in Courier font)
3. **The Editors** — a mysterious editorial body who occasionally interject (footnotes in Times, signed "— Ed." or "— The Editors")

---

## Foreword

- Extremely brief — just a note from "The Editors" saying the first edition lacked Chapter 21, Appendix II, Appendix III, and the Index.
- Final line: **"This is not for you."** — ominous, unexplained. This phrase haunts the whole book.

## Introduction — Johnny Truant's Voice

**Written:** October 31, 1998, from a hotel room in Hollywood, CA. Blood on his hands (not all his).

### Who is Johnny Truant?
- Works as an apprentice at a tattoo shop on Sunset Blvd, Hollywood
- Chaotic, self-destructive lifestyle: drinking, drugs, casual sex, bar-hopping
- Best friend: **Lude** (real name possibly Harry) — hair stylist, knows every bar and bouncer in LA, loves women
- Father died when Johnny was 10. Mother died ~9 years later. Both parents gone.
- Covered in unexplained burn scars on both forearms (wrists to elbows) — he invents stories to explain them. The true story is much darker (hinted at, not revealed here).

### How He Found the Manuscript
- Lude calls him at 3am to come to an old man's apartment on Franklin Ave
- The old man: **Zampanô** — had just died (found face-down, ~80 years old, no trauma, "just old age")
- **Zampanô's apartment:** windows nailed shut and sealed with caulk, vents taped with duct tape — not for security but apparently to *retain the smell* of his things. No clocks, no light bulbs, no food in the fridge — fridge was stuffed with strange pale books.
- Zampanô was **completely blind** since the mid-1950s. Half his books were in Braille. He had female readers visit daily from universities and community centers.
- Found near Zampanô's body: **four deep claw marks/gouges in the hardwood floor**, 6–7 inches long. Police found no cause for them.
- Cats that used to gather in the courtyard had all disappeared in the months before his death — some found dead, most just vanished.
- Lude brought Johnny to see the apartment the night before Goodwill was coming to take everything.

### What Johnny Found
- A black, paint-spattered cedar-lined trunk built in Utica, NY (C.M. Clapp Company) — filled with Zampanô's manuscript.
- **"Reams and reams of it"** — endless fragments: napkins, torn envelopes, even a postage stamp. Handwritten, typed, layered, crossed-out, burned, taped.
- Johnny took the trunk home. At first just curiosity. Then it consumed him — hours vanishing, days lost.

### Zampanô — Key Character Details
- Called himself Zampanô — no ID, no passport, no official documents proving who he was
- No family, no friends, hardly any money
- Walked the courtyard every morning and evening until his death
- Had seven women he would pine for when depressed: **Beatrice, Gabrielle, Anne-Marie, Dominique, Eliane, Isabelle, Claudine**
- Was a **graphomaniac** — wrote until the day before he died; never finished anything
- Left a note dated **January 5, 1997** (day before he died): "Whoever finds and publishes this work shall be entitled to all proceeds. I ask only that my name take its rightful place..."
- His entire project is about a **film that does not exist** — The Navidson Record cannot be found in theaters or video stores. Famous people he quotes deny knowing Navidson or Zampanô. Many cited books are fictional.
- Biggest irony: Zampanô's manuscript is obsessed with *sight*, *light*, *photography*, *space* — yet he had been blind since the 1950s. **He couldn't see a thing.**

### What Happened to Johnny After Reading
- Began to mirror Zampanô: nailed his windows shut, removed his closet and bathroom doors, bought chains and locks, nailed measuring tapes to his floors and walls
- Completely withdrew from the outside world — no Lude, no Thumper, no one could reach him
- By the time of writing (October 31, 1998): in a cheap hotel, blood on his hands, a gun loaded nearby. He threw up in a closet instead of shooting anyone.
- Johnny's warning to the reader: **reading this will change you**. Things will shift. Walls will feel longer. Shadows will feel deeper. "And then the nightmares will begin."

## Key Themes Established
- **Unreliable narration** at every level — fictional film, fictional analysis, chaotic editor
- **Space and interiority** — both architectural and psychological
- **Blindness and sight** — Zampanô's ironic condition
- **Absence and abandonment** — both Navidson and Johnny defined by absent parents
- **Contagion** — the manuscript infects everyone who reads it

---

## Key Contradictions / Things to Watch
- "This is not for you" — who is the intended reader?
- The Editors claim they have never met Johnny Truant — only communicated by letter or rare phone calls. Who are they?
- Zampanô's claw marks were never explained. The cats disappeared. Something happened in that apartment.
- Johnny admits he added the word "water" to one of Zampanô's passages — how much else has he changed?
- Johnny's scars are from something real — not the cult story he tells. What actually happened to him?
