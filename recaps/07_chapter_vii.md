# House of Leaves — Chapter VII Recap

**Epigraph:** Jack London, "To Build A Fire" — about a man with no imagination who does not understand the cold that will kill him.

---

## What This Chapter Is

Chapter VII introduces **the Holloway team**, covers **Explorations #1 through #3**, and traces the mounting domestic tension inside the Navidson home as the house is invaded by strangers and Navidson is forced to watch another man explore his own hallway.

---

## Holloway Roberts — Character Profile

First shot: Holloway steps from a truck carrying a **Weatherby .300 magnum rifle**. The rifle is not incidental — it defines him.

- **Age:** 48
- **Career:** Professional hunter and explorer
- **Background:** Broad, powerful, thick-bearded; consummately driven; possessed of "brassball courage"
- **Introduced** after Navidson told Reston about Karen's demand that he not explore the hallway further ("Exploration A" — a solo foray Navidson made between Chapters V and VI, *still not shown to us*)
- Reston had met Holloway 4 years earlier at a Northwestern symposium on arctic gear design; Holloway bought the concept when he saw "The Five and a Half Minute Hallway" tape (possibly Holloway was the one who first distributed it — see FN 86)
- Arrives **with two employees**:
  - **Jed Leeder** — originally from Vineland, NJ; became an outdoorsman after one look at the Cascades; uncanny sense of direction; shy almost to frailty; loves Lyle Lovett; has a Texan fiancée
  - **Wax Hook (Kirby)** — 26; from Aspen, CO; climbing prodigy; dropped out of HS with more peaks than most lifelong climbers; loves drinking and bragging about sex; never brags about climbing; "a rocky face is always better than you"

---

## The Navidson-Holloway Dynamic

From the moment they shake hands: **mutual dislike**.
- Holloway is unnerved by Navidson's distinguished career
- Navidson is privately furious he can't explore his own house; resents Holloway calling the shots
- Holloway flirts openly with Karen; touches her back, makes her laugh. Navidson **physically bumps Holloway aside** with his shoulder, directs his glare at Karen
- On his Hi 8: *"I can't tell you how much I'd like to deviate that fucker's septum."*
- Navidson's skin condition (stress-related fungal infection on his toes, cracked yellow nails, blood-stained socks) flares up — Zampanô reads this as a non-verbal manifestation of his jealousy and frustration

## The Three Explorations

### Exploration #1
- Team: Holloway, Jed, Wax + Hi 8s, halogen lamps, down gear, radios
- Navidson ties **nearly 2 miles of fishing line** to the door and hands the spool to Holloway
- Findings: series of left turns → **endless corridor** → entrance to enormous void with ceiling **at least 200 feet high** → another even larger entrance opening into an even greater void
- Stopped by: (1) ran out of fishing line, and (2) **hear the growl** — a sound Navidson had already warned them about. Holloway turns back to reassess.
- Walls shift on the way out — but fishing line stays intact; they return safely

### Exploration #2 (~8 hours)
- 4 spools of fishing line, flares, neon markers; Jed collects wall scratchings in sample jars
- The **growl** occurs once; shifts are negligible — *continued use seems to deter the growl and preserve the path*
- Major discovery radioed back: **the Great Hall** — ceiling at least 500 feet high, span approaching a mile
- And then: **a Spiral Staircase** in the center, over 200 feet in diameter, spiraling down into nothing. Flares dropped — never heard to hit bottom
- Navidson has to hand Reston the radio; can no longer pretend to be cheerful. He leaves the room.
- Karen watches Holloway's presentation, engrossed. Tom pulls her aside; tries to get her to let Navidson lead the next one. Karen's response:
  > *"I want to grow old, Tom. I want to grow old with him. Is that such an awful thing?"*
- Tom tells Navidson to find his son. Navidson spends an hour tracking down Chad (who was in the park filling jars with fireflies). They come home with fireflies and ice cream. A small moment of repair.

### Exploration #3 (~20 hours)
- 45 minutes to reach the Spiral Staircase → 7 hours walking *down* it. Flares still show no bottom.
- Staircase diameter has grown from 200 ft to **over 500 ft**
- Return: 11+ hours. All three come back cold, depleted, enthusiasm gone
- Jed: vertigo for the first time — had to step back from the edge. Wax: cavalier but more exhausted than the others
- Holloway: *"It's impossible to photograph what we saw."* — He who believed in documentation admits the house defeats the camera.

## Karen's Compass Discovery

While the men are in Exploration #2, Karen uses a compass (from a Feng Shui master recommended by her mother) to try to realign the house's energy:
- Compass **refuses to settle on any direction** inside the house. She exchanges it. Same result.
- Tom confirms. Holloway confirms it holds inside the hallway too: *"I'll be damned."*
- **North does not exist inside the house.** Holloway has been navigating using only neon arrows, fishing line, and Jed's unerring sense of direction.

## Preparation for Exploration #4

- Tom prepares to leave (hasn't worked in 3+ months); agrees to stay through Exploration #4 when Navidson begs him
- Reston arranges a week off from UVA; stays at the house
- Holloway, Jed, Wax stay at a local motel (until Exploration #4)
- Both Navidson and Holloway **expect fame and fortune** from the discovery — this brings them briefly closer together
- Holloway's growing paranoia (not visible in the film): he fears Navidson will cut him out and claim all the credit
- Chad is getting into fights at school. Returns home with a bruised eye and swollen nose. Refuses to speak about it.

## Johnny Truant's Footnotes — Key Content

### FN 98 — Kyrie (German translation scene)
- Lude has a German friend named **Kyrie** — tall, blonde, speaks Chinese, Japanese, French; triathlete, corporate consultant, drinks quarts of beer
- Johnny met her 5 months earlier at a bar when a huge man ("prehistoric shithead") shoved Johnny for talking to her. Johnny nearly fought him despite the size difference: *"'You got a death wish Truant?' Which was the thing that scared me. 'Cause maybe I did."*
- Second meeting arranged by Lude at Union bar; they take Ecstasy and drive Kyrie's BMW at 90mph on Mulholland Drive; end up at a secluded spot under a street lamp... and have sex (detailed). Johnny: *"Too bad dark languages rarely survive."*
- Kyrie bursts into tears afterward — her boyfriend is in Gdansk (Poland), indifferent to her
- **Punchline**: Johnny forgot to ask her the German translation. (Editors' note: the Rilke line = "But here within this thick black pelt, your strongest gaze will be absorbed and utterly disappear.")
- Johnny now can't bring himself to dial even 7 numbers. Phone is in front of him but "out of reach." Sleep is becoming impossible.

### FN 104 — The Truth About Raymond (Foster Care)
The most direct account yet of Johnny's childhood trauma:
- After his father died at 10, was moved between multiple foster homes (was "trouble wherever I went")
- Ended up with **Raymond** — former marine, "total control freak" with "a beard rougher than horse hide and hands harder than horn"
- Age 12: Raymond woke Johnny in the middle of the night: *"Beast... you're not really in this family but you're living with this family"*
- Johnny fought — at school, every day for a week, against older kids — until something "really poisonous got inside me and turned off all the pain"
- Got expelled; fractured a kid's jaw; received serious injuries himself
- Raymond drove him somewhere before the hospital — *"an ice covered place, surrounded by barbed wire and willows, where monuments of rust, seldom touched, lie frozen alongside fence posts and no one ever comes near enough to hear the hawks cry"* — where Johnny **lost half his tooth** and "a lot more too." (This is where his broken front tooth came from. The actual act is deliberately left obscured.)

### FN 119 — Alaska (age 13)
- At 13, Johnny worked at a salmon cannery on the Homer Spit, Alaska — approved by a woman at an agency who knew he was underage but didn't care
- In his first weeks, the boat he was on sank in a storm. A young Haitian friend was dragged down with the wreck, a line tangled around his ankle. They heard him scream even after his head went under.
- The captain's response to all loss: **"So?"** — "An awful word but it does harden you. It hardened me."
- This word becomes a lifeline: the SOS / "So?" parallel

## Key Themes

- **The hunter and the house**: Holloway brings a rifle into a place that has no prey — yet. His weapon defines how he will eventually respond to the unknown.
- **Naming as possession**: Navidson is furious that Holloway gets to name things — the Anteroom, the Great Hall, the Spiral Staircase. To name is to claim. Navidson is excluded from naming his own discovery.
- **Domestic erosion**: Every exploration strips the family further. The children are neglected. Karen is triangulated between Navidson and Holloway. Chad is fighting at school.
- **The compass**: North doesn't exist inside the house. Human instruments of orientation fail completely.

## Contradictions / Things to Watch

- Navidson's "Exploration A" (solo, before Chapter VI) is referenced but still not shown. We know Karen found out and it broke the promise he made her in Chapter V.
- The fishing line is crucial — they will run out of it, eventually. Note what happens when it's gone.
- FN 86: Holloway may have been the one who originally distributed "The Five and a Half Minute Hallway" tape — making him partly responsible for the film's public exposure
- The growl: Zampanô notes it sounds like *"the rustle of a high mountain wind in the trees"* and that *continued use seems to deter it*. As if the house adapts.
- **Jed's sense of direction is the team's only navigation inside.** When the compass fails and the fishing line runs out, Jed is all they have.
- Zampanô writes of Holloway: *"He feared Navidson would get rid of him and thus deprive him of the recognition he had spent a lifetime trying to obtain."* — This fear will drive Holloway's eventual crack.
