# House of Leaves — Chapter IV Recap

**Epigraph:** "Faith, sir, as to that matter, I don't believe one half of it myself." — Diedrich Knickerbocker

---

## What This Chapter Is

Chapter IV covers the **first major supernatural event**: the discovery of an impossible new room in the house upon the family's return from Seattle. This triggers systematic measurement, the reunion of the estranged brothers, and eventually the revelation that the interior of the house exceeds its exterior dimensions — physically impossible.

---

## The Discovery — Early June 1990

The Navidsons fly to **Seattle for a wedding** (4 days away). When they return:

### The New Room
- Upstairs in the **master bedroom**: a plain white door with a glass knob has appeared where there was only wall before
- Opens into a space like a **walk-in closet**: roughly 5 feet wide, 4 feet long; perfectly smooth walls of **almost pure black** (with a slight grey quality); no outlets, sockets, switches, shelves, molding — nothing
- A second door on the opposite end opens into the **children's bedroom**
- The Hi 8 cameras (motion-detector triggered) show: the family leaves → a week seamlessly skips → they return and the room is already there. The cameras were **never triggered** during their absence.
- Karen digs up old photos proving there was no door before.
- Police are called. **Sheriff Axnard** arrives, files a report, can do nothing: *"Better I guess t'have been a victim of a crazy carpenter than some robber."*
- **Alicia Rosenbaum** (the real estate agent) is baffled.
- Architectural blueprints from 1981 show no room — only a strange crawl space, roughly 4 feet wide, running between both bedrooms. An "ell" addition was planned but never built.

---

## The Measurement Problem

Navidson gets out a measuring tape. Something doesn't add up.

| Measurement | Value |
|-------------|-------|
| External width of house (outside, 100' Empire tape) | 32' 9¾" |
| Internal width (both bedrooms + new space + all walls) | 32' 10" |
| **Discrepancy** | **1/4"** |

The interior is larger than the exterior. By a quarter inch.

He measures six more times. Same result. He drills holes through the outer walls to measure wall thickness precisely. He runs fishing line through the holes, levels it, measures both ways. Still:
> **Interior = 32' 10" exactly. Exterior = 32' 9¾" exactly. The house is 1/4" larger on the inside.**

---

## Tom Arrives

Navidson calls Tom — his estranged fraternal twin — at 4am. Tom drives down from Lowell, Massachusetts.

### Tom — Key Details
- **Fraternal twin** of Will; hasn't spoken to Will in over 8 years
- Builds houses for a living (construction)
- Overweight, affable, funny — *"an innate ability to amuse"*; kids (Chad and Daisy) adore him
- Sober from **alcohol for over 2 years** ("acquaintance of Bill's" = AA reference); still smokes marijuana (which bothers Navidson, who says nothing)
- Their body language around each other: avoidance, the weight of 8 years between words

### Tom Re-Measures
Tom uses a Cowley level mirror transit. He finds Navidson's measurement was slightly off:
> **The discrepancy is not 1/4" — it's 5/16".**

More impossible, not less.

---

## Billy Reston — Consultation at UVA

Both brothers drive to Charlottesville to see **Billy Reston**, a friend who teaches engineering at UVA.

### Reston's Background
- **Paraplegic** — lost the use of his legs in India when a crane operator accidentally cut a high-voltage power cable that fell on him
- Navidson was present and **photographed the moment** — the photo (11x14 B&W) hangs on Reston's office wall: Reston running, the cable descending like a snake
- This is how they became friends — the photo brought them together
- Gruff, caustic, more drill sergeant than professor; brilliant

### Reston's Verdict
> *"There's no question the problem's with your equipment."*

He loans them a **Stanley Beacon level** and a **Leica laser distance meter** — and offers to come measure himself if the new tools fail to solve it. He's reassuring and dismissive.

---

## Meanwhile: Karen and Audrie

While Will and Tom consult Reston, Karen's friend **Audrie McCullogh** drives down from Washington D.C. They build bookshelves together. Karen calls her books her *"newly found day to day comfort"* — she's trying to make the space *normal*.

Karen: *"No better book ends than two walls."* — innocent then; soon ironic.

---

## Return and Confirmation

With Reston's precision instruments, the laser fires across the house:
- Navidson hoots — it looks like the discrepancy has finally been solved
- Tom wants to prop the closet door open; reaches for the biggest book on Karen's shelf
- The books fall like dominoes — but instead of stopping at the wall, the last few **fall to the floor**, revealing at least **a foot of space** between the end of the shelf and the plaster

The wall has moved. The house has grown.

**Karen screams.** (Chapter ends here)

## Johnny Truant's Footnotes — Key Content

### The Tattoo Shop Panic (FN 33)
After copying Heidegger's passage on *Unheimlichkeit* ("not-being-at-home"), Johnny goes to work and has a total breakdown:
- Air thickens; darkness descends though there's no power failure
- He smells something **bitter, foul, inhuman, reeking with rot** — *"telling me in the language of nausea that I'm not alone"*
- Something is behind him. He doesn't look. Then he does — whipping around fast enough to cause whiplash
- Nothing there. The smell gone. The lights normal. But his hands are shaking.

> *"Focus on these words, and whatever you do don't let your eyes wander past the perimeter of this page..."*

This is the book directly addressing the reader — warning them something is behind *them* too.

### Meeting Amber Rightacre (FN 41)
- Johnny finds Zampanô's notepad crammed with names and phone numbers — all his female readers
- Calls several; **Amber Rightacre** (a UC student, quarter French/quarter Native American, dark blue eyes) picks up
- She read **Tolstoy** to Zampanô — particularly *War and Peace* (the French passages she could handle)
- Zampanô once told her: *"I didn't have any children any more."* Then added: *"Of course, you're all my children"* — even though she was alone in the room. She felt surrounded by ghosts.
- She stopped visiting eventually; still feels guilty about it
- Night ends with Amber, her friend Christina (coke, wine, sex) — Johnny forgets everything for a while

### Johnny's Father (buried in the Amber footnote)
A sudden, devastating flashback: watching his father fly his plane, age 8. Then the image of a newspaper photo of a crash — a burning truck/plane, a "jack-knifed trailer." A 10-year-old boy tracing the ink of the accident photo trying to *retract* his father's death. Officials took the clippings away. Washed all the ink from his hands.

> *"Not a word of his own, illegible or otherwise, no god at all, and so by dissolving the black sky bring back the blue. But he never did."*

### FN 36 — The Coffee Spill (and Zampanô's remains)
Defending Zampanô's digressive writing style:
> *"I'm struck by the fact that everything Zampanô had is really gone... a bowl of betel nuts on his mantle... a battered shotgun bearing the initials RLB under his bed... even the curiously preserved bud of a white rose hidden in the drawer of his nightstand."*

Flaze (the building manager) took the shotgun. The rest is gone.
> *"Error and written errors are often the only markers left by a solitary life: to sacrifice them is to lose the angles of personality, the riddle of a soul."*

## Key Themes

- **Unheimlich / the uncanny**: The house becomes literally "not home" — spatially violated
- **Physics as religion**: *"God for all intents and purposes is an equal sign"* — but the house breaks the equation
- **Patience vs. urgency**: Karen's shelf-building (patience/denial) vs. Navidson's obsessive measuring (urgency/reason)
- **Fraternal reunion through crisis**: The anomaly is the only pretext Will could find to call Tom. *"Funny how all it took was a fraction of an inch to get us in a car together."*

---

## Contradictions / Things to Watch

- The new closet appeared while motion-sensor cameras were running — and were **never triggered**. The cameras should have caught something.
- Discrepancy shifts from 1/4" to 5/16" — the house may be *actively growing*
- The "Exploration #4" short showed bloodshed and Daisy crying — but these early chapters show a normal family. The horror is still ahead.
- Johnny's gun (loaded, in the hotel room per Introduction) — his instability mirrors the house's instability
- **FN 45**: A footnote about Fred de Stabenrath is deliberately obscured — Zampanô buried the text under a dark ink spill. Johnny could not recover it. Something is being hidden at the manuscript level.
- The initials **RLB** on the shotgun under Zampanô's bed — never explained
- The **white rose bud** preserved in Zampanô's nightstand — never explained; likely connects to his seven women
